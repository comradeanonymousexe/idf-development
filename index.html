About IDF

A Forum Built on
Human Dignity

Integrated Development Forum (IDF) is a non-governmental, non-political humanitarian and development organisation that has been operating in Bangladesh since 2007. IDF is dedicated to supporting the most vulnerable — from orphans and street children to women, persons with disabilities, and climate-displaced communities.

Supported by international donors including HRF-UK, Islamic Help-UK, Muslim Charity-UK, OSI-USA, and Relief Foundation-Qatar, as well as the Government of Bangladesh, IDF delivers integrated, community-centred solutions at scale.

Our Mission

"To break the cycle of poverty and empower communities to achieve self-reliance, dignity, and a confident, self-reliant society through inclusive and sustainable development."

What We Do

Eight Pillars of Community Change

Every program is designed to be sustainable, community-led, and aligned with SDG goals — because lasting change cannot be imposed from above.

WASH
Program 01
WASH for Life
Safe water through tube wells, hand pumps, and piped networks. Twin-pit latrines and behavioural change communication — serving 120,000 households in Chattogram with World Bank & AIIB funding.
Learn More
Health
Program 02
Health & Nutrition
Static and satellite clinics, telehealth, health camps, and maternal health awareness. Free medical care for the poorest across rural and peri-urban Bangladesh.
Learn More
Education
Program 03
Education & Skills
Non-formal education, adult literacy, disability inclusion in schooling, and vocational skills development for youth — breaking intergenerational poverty through knowledge.
Learn More
Livelihoods
Program 04
Livelihoods & Agriculture
Homestead gardening, livestock rearing, income-generation training, and market linkage — building pathways out of poverty for farming households and smallholder entrepreneurs.
Learn More
Climate
Program 05
Climate Adaptation
Disaster risk reduction, resilient homestead support for coastal communities, solar cold-storage innovation, and social forestation — climate resilience as a development right.
Learn More
Women
Program 06
Women & Child Protection
VGD training, economic empowerment, and protection for women. Dedicated care, education, food, healthcare, and shelter for orphaned and street children across Bangladesh.
Learn More
Disaster
Program 07
Humanitarian & Disaster
Emergency flood and cyclone response, Ramadan food parcel distribution, Eid gifts, Qurbani meat distribution, and year-round relief for the most vulnerable families.
Learn More
PWD/Refugee
Program 08
PWD & Refugee Support
Inclusive development for persons with disabilities, and targeted humanitarian response for Rohingya refugees in Cox's Bazar — dignity without exception.
